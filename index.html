Work Completed So Far
Chronological summary of all SWOT-related work from Oct 2025 to present
COMPLETED — Oct 2025
Ian analyzed long-term wave variability using NDBC buoy data (1980s-present). Monthly averages, climatology, and standard error computed. Discovered discrepancies between NDBC and GLOSS data sources (GLOSS truncated in some periods). Both sources follow similar trends.
COMPLETED — Oct 2025
Ian downloaded archived Donilan data (2010-2021). Started SWOT co-location without corrections. L2 LR (2 km resolution) identified as the right product (not L3). Beta correction needed for orbit-accumulated errors.
COMPLETED — Nov 2025
Team established SWOT-buoy co-location approach. Ian to ID SWOT passes intersecting buoy locations. Michigan preferred for SWOT analysis (good satellite pass alignment with lake shape). Kate identified days with wave heights >2m for SWOT targeting. HRRR wind data sharing initiated.
COMPLETED — Nov 2025
Ian analyzed statistical comparisons between buoy data and model outputs. July and December data for buoy 40507 showed less linear correlations. ERDAP database issue identified and resolved. WaveWatch data explored for SWH comparisons.
COMPLETED — Dec 2025
Ian collected and analyzed MVDC station buoy data. Filtering: distance <2.5 km from closest pixel, time diff within ~15 min. Added RMSE and bias calculations. Uploaded Jupyter notebooks to GitHub. Compared WaveWatch3 model data with buoy measurements.
KEY FINDING — Dec 2025
SWOT accuracy is inversely proportional to SWH: performs poorly at low wave heights (<1m), many zero values when buoy <1m. Accuracy also affected by across-swath position (edges worse than center). Lake Michigan has best data-to-zeros ratio. Lake Superior has fewest zeros overall.
COMPLETED — Dec 2025
July and October selected as target analysis months. October: higher waves, good buoy coverage before winter removal. July: summer baseline. CSV prep planned for Alejandro with buoy coords, SWOT coords, timestamps. Bounding box approach for data extraction.
DECISION — Dec 2025
SWOT doesn't need to be central to Chapter 1. Dissertation focus may shift from being purely "winter waves." SWOT is its own project track. Version D data (with beta correction for antenna heating) identified as the target version.
PENDING — Jan 2026
Task created: Download SWOT Version D Windwave data (SWOT_L2_LR_SSH_Windwave_D) from May 2025 onward for GL bounding box. Status: Next Action. High priority.
PENDING
Waiting on Alejandro for processed SWOT data with coastal corrections. Follow-up needed — Alejandro is busy. Team can reach out directly when Bia is away.

1
Spatial Validation of WW3

Buoys give point comparisons. SWOT gives 2D SWH maps that can be directly overlaid on WW3 gridded output. Produce spatial bias maps (model - SWOT) instead of just bias at 5-8 buoy locations.

Key question: Is the +0.28 m bias uniform or does it have spatial structure (fetch-dependent? nearshore vs offshore)?

Connects to: Winter Validation project

2
Satellite-Observed Fetch-Limited Growth

Kate's gustiness work found C(fetch) varies 6x along a Michigan transect — but that's all model-derived. SWOT can provide observed along-fetch SWH profiles. Pick passes where wind is roughly cross-swath to get empirical fetch-growth curves.

Potential first: First satellite-observed fetch-limited growth curves in the Great Lakes.

Connects to: Gustiness project

3
Gustiness Crossover Spatial Verification

Finding 3 (negative enhancement at short fetch / western shore) is currently model + limited buoy evidence. Classify SWOT passes by gustiness using HRRR, then check whether spatial SWH patterns near the upwind shore match model predictions.

Key question: Do gusty conditions show suppressed SWH at short fetch compared to steady conditions?

Connects to: Gustiness project
